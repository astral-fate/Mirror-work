                <!-- Day 1 -->

                <div class="day">

                    <button class="day-toggle">Day 1: Loving Yourself</button>

                    <div class="day-content">

                        <h3>Your Day 1 Mirror Work Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>Stand or sit in front of your bathroom mirror.</li>

                            <li>Look into your eyes.</li>

                            <li>Take a deep breath and say this affirmation: <em>I want to like you. I want to really learn to love you. Let’s go for it and really have some fun.</em></li>

                            <li>Take another deep breath and say, <em>I’m learning to really like you. I’m learning to really love you.</em></li>

                            <li>This is the first exercise, and I know it can be a little challenging, but please stay with it. Keep taking deep breaths. Look into your eyes. Use your own name as you say, <em>I’m willing to learn to love you, [Name]. I’m willing to learn to love you.</em></li>

                            <li>Throughout the day, each time you pass a mirror or see your reflection, please repeat these affirmations, even if you have to do it silently.</li>

                        </ol>

                        <p><em>At first when you do your mirror work, you may feel silly or stupid repeating the affirmations. You may even be angry or want to cry. That’s all right—in fact, it’s quite normal. And you’re not alone. Remember that I’m right here with you. I’ve been through this, too. And tomorrow is a new day.</em></p>

 

                        <h3>The Power Is Within You: Your Day 1 Journaling Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>After you finish your morning mirror work, write down your feelings and observations. Did you feel angry or upset or silly?</li>

                            <li>Six hours after finishing your morning mirror work, again write down your feelings and observations. As you continued to practice your mirror work formally and informally, did you start to believe what you were saying to yourself?</li>

                            <li>Keep track of any changes in your behavior or your beliefs over the course of the day. Did the exercise get easier, or did you continue to find it difficult after doing it awhile?</li>

                            <li>At the end of the day, before you go to bed, write down what you learned from doing your mirror work.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>Your Heart Thought for Day 1: I Am Open and Receptive</h3>

                        <p>If we do mirror work to create good in our lives but there’s a part of us that doesn’t believe we are worth it, we are not going to believe the words we are saying as we look in the mirror. We will reach a point at which we start thinking, <em>Mirror work doesn’t work.</em></p>

                        <p>The truth is, the perception that mirror work doesn’t work has nothing to do with the mirror work itself or the affirmations we say. The problem is that we don’t believe we deserve all the good that life has to offer.</p>

                        <p>If that’s your belief, then affirm: <em>I am open and receptive.</em></p>

                    </div>

                </div>

 

                <!-- Day 2 -->

                <div class="day">

                    <button class="day-toggle">Day 2: Making Your Mirror Your Friend</button>

                    <div class="day-content">

                        <h3>Your Day 2 Mirror Work Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>Stand in front of your bathroom mirror.</li>

                            <li>Look into your eyes.</li>

                            <li>Using your name, say this affirmation: <em>[Name], I love you. I really, really love you.</em></li>

                            <li>Take a few moments now to say it two or three more times: <em>I really, really love you, [Name].</em></li>

                            <li>Keep repeating this affirmation over and over. I want you to be able to say it at least 100 times a day. Yes, that’s right: 100 times a day. I know that seems like a lot, but honestly, 100 times a day is easy once you get into the swing of it.</li>

                            <li>So each time you pass a mirror or see your reflection just repeat this affirmation: <em>[Name], I love you. I really, really love you.</em></li>

                        </ol>

                         <p><em>When you find it difficult to tell yourself I love you, it is most likely because you are judging yourself, repeating those old, negative messages. Don’t add to your discomfort by judging yourself for making judgments. Just relax and commit to saying the affirmation. Remember: you are committing to an affirmation that’s true. The truth is, we do love ourselves, when we are not judging ourselves.</em></p>

 

                        <h3>The Power Is Within You: Your Day 2 Journaling Exercise</h3>

                        <p>Life is very simple. What we give out, we get back. In your journal, write down your responses to the following questions:</p>

                        <ol>

                             <li>What do you want that you are not getting?</li>

                             <li>When you were growing up, what were the rules about deserving? Did you always have to earn in order to deserve? Were things taken away from you when you did something wrong?</li>

                             <li>Do you feel that you deserve to live? To have joy? If not, why not?</li>

                        </ol>

                        <p><em>As you answer these questions, notice the emotions you are feeling. Write them down in your journal.</em></p>

 

                        <h3>Your Heart Thought for Day 2: I Am Deserving</h3>

                        <p>Sometimes we refuse to put any effort into creating a good life for ourselves because we believe that we don’t deserve it. The belief that we don’t deserve it may come from our early-childhood experiences. We might be buying into someone else’s concept or opinion that has nothing to do with our own reality.</p>

                        <p>Deserving has nothing to do with being good. It is our unwillingness to receive the good in life that gets in the way. Allow yourself to accept the good, whether you think you deserve it or not.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

 

                <!-- Day 3 -->

                 <div class="day">

                    <button class="day-toggle">Day 3: Monitoring Your Self-Talk</button>

                    <div class="day-content">

                        <h3>Your Day 3 Mirror Work Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>Stand or sit in front of your bathroom mirror.</li>

                            <li>Look into your eyes.</li>

                            <li>Say this affirmation: <em>Whatever I say to myself, I will say it with love.</em></li>

                            <li>Keep repeating it: <em>Whatever I say to myself in this mirror, I will say it with love.</em></li>

                            <li>Is there a statement that you heard as a child that still sticks in your head? Maybe something like “You’re stupid” or “You’re not good enough,” or whatever else comes to mind. Take time to work with negative statements and turn them into positive affirmations: <em>I am smart. I am more intelligent than I realize. I am a genius with an abundance of creative ideas. I am a magnificent person. I am lovable. I am worth loving.</em></li>

                            <li>Choose one or two of these new, positive affirmations and say them over and over again. Keep saying them until you feel comfortable with them.</li>

                            <li>Each time you pass a mirror or see your reflection in a window throughout the day, stop and repeat these loving affirmations.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>The Power Is Within You: Your Day 3 Journaling Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>Did you repeat a negative story today? Write down how many times you repeated it and how many people you told it to. Now write down something positive you can tell those people tomorrow that will help them feel better about themselves and everyone around them.</li>

                            <li>Write down the word <em>should</em>. Next to it, make a list of words to use instead. You might start with the word <em>could</em>.</li>

                            <li>Post several of the new, positive affirmations you learned today on your mirror so you can practice them whenever you see them.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>Your Heart Thought for Day 3: I Always Have a Choice</h3>

                        <p>Most of us have foolish ideas about who we are and many rigid rules about how life should be lived. Let’s remove the word <em>should</em> from our vocabulary forever. <em>Should</em> is a word that makes prisoners of us. Every time we use <em>should</em>, we are making ourselves wrong or someone else wrong. We are, in effect, saying: <em>not good enough</em>.</p>

                        <p>What can be dropped now from your <em>should</em> list? Replace the word <em>should</em> with the word <em>could</em>. <em>Could</em> lets you know that you have a choice, and choice is freedom. We need to be aware that everything we do in life is done by choice. There is really nothing we <em>have</em> to do. We always have a choice.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

 

                 <!-- Day 4 -->

                 <div class="day">

                    <button class="day-toggle">Day 4: Letting Go of Your Past</button>

                    <div class="day-content">

                        <h3>Your Day 4 Mirror Work Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>Stand in front of your bathroom mirror.</li>

                            <li>Take a deep breath and, as you exhale, allow all the tension to leave your body.</li>

                            <li>Look at your forehead and imagine that you are pressing a button that ejects a disk of all the old beliefs and negative thoughts that have been playing in your head. Reach up and imagine that you are pulling this recording out of your head and throwing it away.</li>

                            <li>Now look deeply into your eyes and tell yourself, <em>Let’s make a new recording of positive beliefs and affirmations.</em></li>

                            <li>Say these affirmations aloud: <em>I am willing to let go. I release. I let go. I release all tension. I release all fear. I release all anger. I release all guilt. I release all sadness. I let go of old limitations and beliefs. I let go, and I am at peace. I am at peace with myself. I am at peace with the process of life. I am safe.</em></li>

                            <li>Repeat these affirmations two or three times.</li>

                            <li>Throughout the day, whenever any difficult thoughts arise, take out your pocket mirror and repeat these affirmations. Become familiar with them so that repeating them becomes a part of your daily routine.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>The Power Is Within You: Your Day 4 Journaling Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>I find that most of the problems in our lives are caused by what I call the Big Four: Criticism, Fear, Guilt, and Resentment. In your journal, create four columns, heading each with one of the Big Four categories. Think about what role they play in your life. Write your thoughts and feelings about each category in the appropriate column.</li>

                            <li>From Step 1, take the two categories you wrote the most about and write down ten positive affirmations in each category. For example, if one of the categories is Resentment, you might write affirmations like <em>I now choose to release all hurt and resentment. The more resentment I release, the more love I have to give.</em></li>

                            <li>Everything in our lives is a mirror of who we are. Think of the people in your life who are the most challenging for you. What are the traits that bother you most about them? Write those down.</li>

                            <li>Look at the traits that you listed in Step 3. Write down how each of these traits mirrors a belief that you have about yourself. You might also want to jot down what you have learned about yourself in doing today’s exercises.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>Your Heart Thought for Day 4: I Can Let Go</h3>

                         <p>We create habits and patterns because they serve us in some way. It is amazing how many illnesses we create because we want to punish a parent. We may not be doing this consciously—in fact, in most cases it is not conscious. But when we start looking within, we find the pattern. We often create negativity because we do not know how to handle some area of life. If that’s the case, ask yourself: <em>What am I feeling sorry about? Who am I angry at? What am I trying to avoid? How do I think this will this save me?</em></p>

                         <p>If you are not ready to let something go—you really want to hold on to it because it serves you—it doesn’t matter what you do; you will not be able to let it go. However, when you are ready to let something go, it is amazing how easy it is to release it.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

 

                 <!-- Day 5 -->

                 <div class="day">

                    <button class="day-toggle">Day 5: Building Your Self-Esteem</button>

                    <div class="day-content">

                        <h3>Your Day 5 Mirror Work Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>Stand in front of your bathroom mirror.</li>

                            <li>Look into your eyes.</li>

                            <li>Say this affirmation: <em>I love and approve of myself.</em></li>

                            <li>Keep saying it over and over again: <em>I love and approve of myself.</em></li>

                            <li>Repeat this affirmation at least 100 times a day. Yes, that’s right: 100 times. Let <em>I love and approve of myself</em> become your mantra.</li>

                            <li>Each time you pass a mirror or see your reflection, repeat this affirmation.</li>

                        </ol>

                         <p><em>This is an exercise I have given to hundreds and hundreds of people over the years. The results are absolutely phenomenal when people stick with it. Remember: mirror work doesn’t work in theory; it only works in practice. If you do it, it really will make a difference.</em></p>

                         <p><em>If any negative thoughts come up—such as How can I approve of myself when I am fat?, or It’s silly to think that I can think this way, or I am no good—don’t resist them, don’t fight them, don’t judge them. Let them just be there. Stay focused on what you really want to experience, which is to love and approve of yourself. You can gently let go of other thoughts that intrude and stay focused on I love myself and approve of myself.</em></p>

                         <p><em>What we are doing in mirror work is trying to get back to the heart of who we really are. We want to experience who we are when we are not judging ourselves.</em></p>

 

                        <h3>The Power Is Within You: Your Day 5 Journaling Exercise</h3>

                        <ol>

                            <li>Write down some of the ways you don’t love yourself or ways you express your lack of self-worth. Do you criticize your body? Do you talk down to yourself?</li>

                            <li>Write down some of the negative opinions you think others have of you. For each of these negative opinions, write down an affirmation to turn it into a positive statement. For example, you could change <em>My mother thinks I’m fat</em> to <em>I am beautiful exactly as I am.</em></li>

                            <li>Make a list of all the reasons why you love yourself. Make another list of why people love to spend time with you.</li>

                            <li>Post these loving lists where you can see them every day.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>Your Heart Thought for Day 5: I Love Being Me</h3>

                        <p>Can you imagine how wonderful it would be if you could live your life without ever being criticized by anyone? Wouldn’t it be wonderful to feel totally at ease, totally comfortable? You would get up in the morning and know you were going to have a wonderful day, because everybody would love you and nobody would put you down. You would feel just great.</p>

                        <p>You know what? You can give this to yourself. You can make the experience of living with yourself the most wonderful experience imaginable. You can wake up in the morning and feel the joy of spending another day with you.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

 

                 <!-- Day 6 -->

                 <div class="day">

                    <button class="day-toggle">Day 6: Releasing Your Inner Critic</button>

                    <div class="day-content">

                         <h3>Your Day 6 Mirror Work Exercise</h3>

                         <ol>

                            <li>Find a quiet place with a mirror where you will feel safe and where you won’t be disturbed.</li>

                            <li>Look into the mirror. Look straight into your eyes. If you are still uncomfortable doing this, then concentrate on your mouth or your nose. Talk to your inner child. Your inner child wants to grow and blossom, and needs love, acceptance, and praise.</li>

                            <li>Now say these affirmations: <em>I love you. I love you, and I know you are doing the best you can. You are perfect just as you are. I approve of you.</em></li>

                            <li>You may want to do this exercise several times before you truly feel that your inner voice is less critical. Do what feels right for you.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>The Power Is Within You: Your Day 6 Journaling Exercise</h3>

                         <ol>

                            <li>Make a list of five things you criticize yourself for.</li>

                            <li>Go down the list, and beside each item write the date on which you began to criticize yourself for this particular thing. If you can’t remember the exact date, approximate.</li>

                            <li>Are you amazed at how long you’ve been picking on yourself? This habit of self-criticism has not produced any positive changes, has it? Criticism doesn’t work! It only makes you feel bad. So be willing to stop it.</li>

                            <li>Change each of the five criticisms on your list to a positive affirmation.</li>

                            <li>Carry this list with you. When you notice yourself being judgmental, take out the list of affirmations and read it a few times. Better yet, read it aloud in front of your mirror.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>Your Heart Thought for Day 6: I Love and Accept Myself Exactly as I Am</h3>

                         <p>We all have areas of our lives that we think are unacceptable and unlovable. If we are really angry with parts of ourselves, we often engage in self-abuse. We abuse alcohol, drugs, or cigarettes. We overeat. We beat ourselves up emotionally. One of the worst things we do, which causes more damage than anything else, is criticize ourselves. We need to stop all criticism. Once we get into the habit of not criticizing ourselves, it is amazing how we stop criticizing other people. We realize that everyone is a reflection of us, and what we see in another person we can see in ourselves.</p>

                         <p>When we complain about someone, we are really complaining about ourselves. When we can truly love and accept who we are, there is nothing to complain about. We cannot hurt ourselves, and we cannot hurt another person. Let’s make a vow that we will no longer criticize ourselves for anything.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>

                            <li>Stand in front of your bathroom mirror.</li>

Take your hand and imagine that you are pulling that disk out of your head and throwing it away.</li>

                            <li>Now look deeply in your eyes and imagine that you are making a new CD of positive affirmations: <em>I am willing to let go. I am worth loving. I am perfect just the way I am.</em></li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>The Power Is Within You: Your Day 7 Journaling Exercise</h3>

                         <ol>

                            <li>Take out your journal and open it to the first exercise you did, on Day 1.</li>

                            <li>Read the feelings and observations you wrote down after your mirror work on Day 1.</li>

                            <li>On a new page, write down your feelings and observations after the first week of doing your mirror work. Are the exercises getting easier? Are you feeling more comfortable looking in the mirror?</li>

                            <li>Write down where you are having the most success with your mirror work. Write down where you are having the most difficulty.</li>

                            <li>Create new affirmations to help you in those areas where you are having blocks.</li>

                        </ol>

 

                        <h3>Your Heart Thought for Day 7: All My Experiences Are Right for Me</h3>

                         <p>We have been going through doors since the moment we were born. That was a big door and a big change, and we have been through many doors since then.</p>

                         <p>We came to this lifetime equipped with everything we need in order to live fully and richly. We have all the wisdom and knowledge we need. We have all the abilities and talents we need. We have all the love we need. Life is here to support us and take care of us. We need to know and trust that this is so.</p>

                         <p>Doors are constantly closing and opening, and if we stay centered in ourselves, then we will always be safe, no matter which doorway we pass through. Even when we pass through the last doorway on this planet, it is not the end. It is simply the beginning of another new adventure. Trust that it is all right to experience change.</p>

                         <p>Today is a new day. We will have many wonderful new experiences. We are loved. We are safe.</p>

                <!-- Day 8 -->

                <div class="day">

                    <button class="day-toggle">Day 8: Loving Your Inner Child—Part One</button>

                    <div class="day-content">

                         <h3>Your Day 8 Mirror Work Exercise</h3>

                         <ol>

                            <li>Find a photo of yourself when you were about five years old. Tape the photo to your bathroom mirror.</li>

                            <li>Look at the photo for a few minutes. What do you see? Do you see a happy child? A miserable child?</li>

                            <li>Talk to your inner child in the mirror. You can look at the photo or even look into your own eyes—whichever feels more comfortable for you. If you had a nickname as a child, use this name as you speak to your inner child. What works really well is to sit in front of the mirror, because if you are standing, as soon as difficult feelings start to come up, you might be tempted to run out the door. So sit down, grab a box of tissues, and start talking.</li>

                            <li>Open your heart and share your innermost thoughts.</li>

                       <h3>Your Heart Thought for Day 20: I Communicate Openly with My Children</h3>

                        <p>It is vitally important to keep the lines of communication open with children, especially during the teen years. So often children are told things like <em>Don’t say that.
